Red Rock Canyon is home to many trails and adventurous activities. The trails range from easy to strenuous conditions. According to the Hiking Guide in the Red Rock Canyon Visitor Guide each trail is rated as either easy, moderate, strenuous or a combination of these.

Hike Ratings Guide*:
Strenuous: Generally, lots of uphill sections (and later downhill); possibly more than 1,000 feet of elevation gain; double digit mileage and/ or difficult terrain and rock scrambling.
Moderate: Uphill Sections include up to 1,000 feet of elevation gain; single digit mileage; uneven terrain and some rock scrambling.
Easy: Like a walk in the city, but on uneven terrain. (Not guaranteed to seem easy it you are not used to trail hiking.)
Combination: Listed as easy-moderate, moderate-strenuous and so on.

*Taken directly from the Fall-Winter 2006/2007 Red Rock Canyon Keystone Visitor Guide. A copy of this guide can be obtained at the Red Rock Canyon Visitor Center.
