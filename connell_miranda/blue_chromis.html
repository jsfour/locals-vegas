Blue Green Chromis (Chromis viridis)

 

These little fish can be found in large schools on top of coral, or in sheltered areas such as subtidal reef flats and lagoons of the Indo-Pacific.

This small school can usually be found near the surface in close proximity to our coral outcropping or in our large Elkhorn coral toward the rear of the aquarium.  They primarily feed on phytoplankton.

Males are in charge of  preparing the nest for spawning, as well as protecting itguarding it predators and ventilating the fertilized eggs by waiving his tail over them in a "fanning" motion.
