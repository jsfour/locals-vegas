A massive, 3-alarm fire erupted at the Moulin Rouge on May 29, 2003. Investigators have ruled out the idea that the fire was an accident. Arson was cited as the cause of the fire.

The fire was set in the middle of the night. Local residents watched as fire fighters tried to put out the flames. It took several hours before the fire was completely extinguished.

The fire spread rapidly due to the fact that the building did not have properly working sprinklers. Due to the fact that that the building was not in use, it did not have to meet building codes that required working sprinklers.
