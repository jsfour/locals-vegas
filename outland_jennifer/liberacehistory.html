Liberace History

 

Walter Valentino Liberace was born 1919 in West Allis, Wisconsin.

Liberace is Polish and Italian with a musical background starting with his parents.

Liberace received a scholarship to the Wisconsin College of Music.

Liberace earned two emmy's from a Los Angeles TV show and wrote four books.

He played at Radio City Music Hall doing 21 shows, where he topped the ticket selling record and made over 2 million dollars.

Liberace Passed away February 4, 1987 at 67 years.

The year of 1978 Liberace opened the shopping complex so people can view personal collections that Liberace owned.

The Liberace Museum was opened April 15, 1979.

Liberace opened other businesses including an antique shop, rehearsal room, and a dance studio, which now displays costumes.

Liberace also opened a restaurant called "Trivoli Gardens" May 1983, the food was international consisting of Polish, Italian, German, and Chinese.
