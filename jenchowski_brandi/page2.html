About Mt. Charleston

 

Mt. Charleston is a retreat from the busy city of Las Vegas. It is a savior from the desert heat and a ski resort in the winter. At 11,918 feet above sea level, the mountain is about 25-30 degrees cooler than the city. The Mount Charleston Wilderness contains 18,000 acres of bristlecone pine, the most extensive stand of these ancient trees in the Intermountain Region.

 

 

The Mountain has two prominent canyons, Lee and Kyle. Kyle Canyon offers a lodge, cabins and campgrounds and Lee Canyon is more for activities like skiing, snowbroading, camping, hiking and it offers no gas on its side of the mountain.
