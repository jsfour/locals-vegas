CUISINE

Mediterranean Food is a combination of many different cultures and cuisines around the Mediterranean Sea. The food is influenced by countries like Italy, Greece, parts of Spain, Portugal, Turkey, North Africa especially Morocco and Tunisia, and Southern France. The foods usually emphasis the use of olive oils, less animal protein, more carbohydrates and vegetables, and legumes such as dried beans and peas.

Popular Mediterranean Food and Ingredients

Hummus- is like a bean dip. You normally dip pita bread in the hummus and eat it like a dip. It is made of chickpea paste and tahini (sesame seed paste) with seasoning such as olive oil, garlic, lemon juice and paprika.

Gyro- is a meat product using beef and lamb. These two meats are grounded up together and can be served many ways. This meat is very tender and sweet, you must try it at least once in your life.

Dolmades- is a fruit or vegetable, most of the time it is a grape leaf or cabbage leaf, cooked with a filling of ground meat and herbs or rice.

Falafel- is a fried ball or patty of spiced fava beans or chickpeas. Falafel is kind of like a bean meatball, minus the meat.

Feta Cheese- is a classic curd cheese, made from goat’s and sheep’s milk.

Lentil Soup- Lentil is a vegetable much like a pea. Lentil soup is a lot like pea soup with ingredients like carrots, potatoes, zucchini and onions.

Lavosh Bread- is a thin, flat crisp bread (like pizza crust) used as a wrap.

Pita Bread- is round plat wheat bread with a pocket. It can be stuffed or dipped in hummus.

Baklava- is a rich Greek desert. It is sweet pastry with chopped nuts layered with sweetened sugar or honey.

Cannoli- is an Italian desert. It has a fried breaded outer layer shaped like a tube filled with sweet, creamy filling like whipped cream sometimes topped with chocolate.
