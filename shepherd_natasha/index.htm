The beauty of the nature sanctuary is that it's a laid back place that's away from the glitz and glam of the Strip. You can take your time strolling through the property and observing the animals. Some of them have been donated, many others rescued, and some are the offspring of animals that were there years ago.

You will notice signs that say "bird sanctuary" because that's what it originally was. There are birds of all kinds that find refuge at the sanctuary. But in addition to the chickens, parrots, ostriches, emus and many other species, you will also find farm animals like donkeys, miniature horses, pigs and goats. There are also a few turtles and lizzards . It's a great place to take children so they can learn to appreciate animals.

Keep in mind that it is an animal sanctuary so it smells like one. It's really cheap to get in and the money is used to help run the refuge. It's located near Floyd Lamb State Park, in case you want to see both locations in the same day.
