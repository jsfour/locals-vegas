Trails

Red Rock Canyon Park is a great place to go to do some hiking with the family on some fun trails. There are about twenty different trails you can try. They go from easy to strenuous. Easy is like a walk in the street but with rocks and hills. Moderate hikes contain more uphill hiking and a rougher terrain and possibly some rock scrambling. strenuous is for advanced hikers.

The best trails for your family are the easy to easy-moderate trails depending on the ages of your kids.

Here are two trails that are easy and fun to do with your family because of all the different things you can see along the way. Also, remember these trails are different depending on what season of the year you go. So have fun and be safe

Remember to bring water and use sun screen.

The Lost Creek-Children's Discovery trail is a moderately easy hike that your family can see a variety of plant life and a number of cultural sites. If your family decides to go during the months of January through March you might be able to see a seasonal waterfall.

The picture on the left is where this waterfall would come down in those particular months. The Lost Creek trail is some uphill climbing with a little of rock scrambling. This hike would be good for kids starting at the age of six. If you look really hard you might be able to find the lost creek, pictured to the right.

 

 

 

 

The Willow Springs Loop Trail is an easy hike and only about one hour round trip to do. Your family can start at the Lost Creek Trail and take the detour to do this one. This trail passes over many agave roasting pit sites. This is where the Native Americans use to cook their food in the dirt. The picture above is of a leaning rock that looks like a flaky biscuit. When you family is hiking along the trails try to find different rocks and see what they look like to you.
