To my surprise the friendly staff was able to get my friend and I in for 
two pedicures on a Saturday afternoon. 

 When the nail tech's came and took us back I was amazed to see 
two chairs that looked more like thrones than pedi-chairs that 
you see at every other salons. 

As we sat down and picked out our colors, a receptionist from the 
front came and asked if we would like to have lunch while 
we had our pedicures! Fancy! 

After ordering from a nearby sandwich shop, the nail tech's took 
out what looked like perfume bottles and said  that we were
 going on a "Sensory Journey". 

I must admit my friend and I were a little freaked out at the term, 
but quickly relaxed when we were told that we were just picking
out the aroma that would be used during our service.
 The nail tech had us close our eyes as the waved the different
 bottles of fragrance under our noses. 

There were scents of musk, citrus, floral and spice. 
After choosing our scents the pedicure began, and our food came 
shortly after.The nail tech began scrub my feet with exfoliate scrubbing off the
 accumulated dead skin. She then rinsed off my feet and
 proceeded to put a mask that smelled of peppermint on my feet 
and let it sit for a couple of minutes. 

As I sat enjoying every last minute of my experience I almost 
forgot about my food! 

After taking off the mask a massage was given, the composition 
that was chosen during my sensory journey was incorporated in 
my massage. It was pretty much the best massage of my life! 
After the massage and the regular maintenance of the pedicure 
I emerged from the chair smelling like sandalwood,
 feeling as if I was floating on air with bright pink toenails!
