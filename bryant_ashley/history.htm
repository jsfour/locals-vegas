Our Story 

Dedicated to Nancy Jane Shaw,Centennial Hills was founded 
on a mission. After Nancy Shaw,  the mother of salons owners,
 Jenny and Dave Lockridge,suffered a stroke that
 left her paralyzed on the right side and confined to a
 wheelchair  in 1997. 

Her family tried to bring a sense of normalcy back 
into her life by taking her to salons to get her
 hair and nails done like before. 
The only difference was that most salons aren't designed
for the handicapped, making maneuvering not only 
difficult, but also a burden on the client as well as service 
providers. 

It wasn't until years of having to move furniture, and 
inconvenience other customers and service providers to
get a service done that Nancy expressed her embarrassment
with her family. 

Joking one evening during the holiday season of 2000. 
Marvin shaw (Nancy's Husband) and Jenny Lockridge
(Marvin and Nancy's daughter) decided “we should just build 
our own salon that could accommodate
 customers with these   needs, so that anyone could be our
 customer. We could create a salon and day spa   
that Las Vegas could be proud of."
Construction started early 2001,There were interviews at
 Marve and Nancy's house and training at Jenny and son-in-laws 
Dave's house . 

The decision of making it an Aveda salon came
 after a meeting with representatives and realizing 
that what Aveda stands for was along 
the same lines of what the family stood for as well.
 Construction ended and the doors of Centennial Hills 
Aveda Salon and Day Spa opened, December 21,2001. 
Nancy Jane Shaw passed away April 9,2005. 

 Her spirit  is felt in the warmth of the staff at Centennial Hills 
Salon and Day Spa and her memory lives on everyday that the 
salon is open.
